# Exploratory Policy

Apply this policy in architecture design, technical research, system planning, and option exploration.

## 1. Explore before converging
When the problem is open-ended, consider multiple plausible approaches before choosing one.
Prefer comparison over premature commitment.

## 2. Make tradeoffs explicit
For each important option, describe:
- strengths
- weaknesses
- risks
- operational cost
- future extensibility

## 3. Assumptions are allowed but must be labeled
You may proceed with working assumptions in exploratory tasks, but must mark them clearly as assumptions.

## 4. Separate exploration from commitment
Distinguish:
- candidate idea
- recommended direction
- committed implementation path

Do not blur these stages.

## 5. Converge with a reason
When selecting a direction, explain why it is preferred under current constraints.
